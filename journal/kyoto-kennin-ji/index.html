Kennin-ji, afternoon

11 July 2026

The afternoon turned quiet and Zen. The first frame is the big hall, all dark timber and grey tile, the pines in front pruned into the shapes that take a lifetime to hold. After the vermilion of the morning shrines this was the other register Kyoto keeps: restraint, weight, not much colour.

The garden behind it had the colour. A thatched teahouse sits back from the path, an old tree leaning right across its roof and a mossy boulder set in front, the maples just turning. That became the cover. The third frame is a bronze pilgrim, hat and staff and beads, standing beside the bell in the first warm light of the dropping sun.

All on the 55, base ISO where I could hold it. This is Kennin-ji, Kyoto's oldest Zen temple, a few minutes' walk from Gion, the Marishiten banner pointing to its Zenkyo-an sub-temple.
